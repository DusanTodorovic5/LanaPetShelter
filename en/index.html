How to properly care for a cat?

A cat can live in an apartment and be perfectly happy without going outside, and if you live in a traffic-free environment and your cat is healthy, neutered, vaccinated, resourceful, nimble and if she is used to this kind of life, you can let your cat out. If you live in an apartment, it is important to keep the cat in safe conditions - fence the balcony or terrace with a net, look out the windows and similar. Cats should be provided with toys and at least once a day you should play with them, preferably before bedtime, with a ball or laser, so that the cat could expend energy and sleep peacefully at night. The cat is an exclusive carnivore and although it stopped hunting as a pet, its nutritional needs have not changed. It is most practical to feed your cat dehydrated cat food, because it contains all the necessary nutrients. If you feed your cat food prepared at home, the meal should consist mostly of meat food (chicken, turkey, beef and blue fish). Cats need foods rich in protein, fiber, vitamins, minerals and fats.

What does animal walfare represent?

Animal welfare is a complex issue that has its scientific, ethical, economic, cultural, social, religious and political dimensions. Caring for animal welfare is based on the belief that animals are sentient beings and that it is therefore important to pay attention to whether they are in good condition or suffering, especially when they are cared for by humans. Caring for animal welfare includes considering the ways in which animals are kept (as pets, in zoos, on farms, in circuses ...), then the ways in which animals are slaughtered for food, animals are used in scientific research, such as and the ways in which human activities affect the welfare and survival of wildlife. Animal welfare means the physical and mental condition of the animal, in relation to the conditions in which it lives and dies. An animal is in a good state of well-being when it is healthy, fed, safe, able to display behaviors important to its physical and mental condition, if it is comfortable and when it does not suffer from unpleasant conditions such as pain, fear and stress. A good state of welfare includes disease prevention and adequate veterinary care, provision of shelter, nutrition, a stimulating and safe environment, humane treatment and humane slaughter or deprivation of life. In Serbia, animal welfare is defined as providing conditions in which animals can meet their physiological and other needs specific to the species, such as nutrition and nutrition, accommodation, physical, mental and thermal comfort, safety, basic behaviors, social contact with animals of the same species, absence of unpleasant experiences such as pain, suffering, fear, stress, illness and injury.
